This clock, which is a typical icon of the city of Padang, is located in Bukittinggi and has been around since the 1920s. Built with a mechanically rotating movement, this watch was one of the most advanced of its era. You will not pay a penny of retribution fees to be able to enter this area. For parking fees, the local community usually charges Rp. 2,000.00 for motorbikes and Rp. 5,000.00 for cars. Apart from being crowded during the day, this place is highly recommended to visit at night because the many lights will beautify this one building.

Mentawai Island


Click here if you want to know more about Pulau Mentawai

Mentawai Islands Regency is one of the regencies located in the province of West Sumatra, Indonesia. This district is outside the territory of the island of Sumatra, consisting of four main islands. This district consists of 4 main inhabited island groups namely Siberut Island, Sipora Island, North Pagai Island and South Pagai Island which are inhabited by the majority of the Mentawai people.

Istana Basa Pagaruyung


Click here if you want to know more about Istana Basa Pagaruyung

The local people used to call this place the word palace or palace. This place is a replica of the original palace which was burnt down during the Padri war in the 1800s. The entry fee is around IDR 15,000.00 for adult visitors and IDR 7,000.00 for children. If you come on certain dates, you can enjoy traditional dances held by residents in this place. In this place, you can rent traditional Minang clothes and wear them to go around the Basa Palace for a certain time. To capture your moments here, you can also rent photo services which are widely scattered around the Basa Palace area. For those of you who are travelers at heart, this place offers a camping ground that you can use to spend the night with friends. Camping in this place can be a new experience for you, apart from being in the mountains where there is still little light.
